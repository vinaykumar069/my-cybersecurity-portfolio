My First CTF Write-up

Posted on October 26, 2023

I recently participated in my first Capture The Flag (CTF) competition, and it was an exhilarating experience. I decided to document my journey, the challenges I faced, and the things I learned along the way.

The Beginning

The CTF was a jeopardy-style competition with a variety of categories, including web exploitation, reverse engineering, cryptography, and forensics. As a beginner, I decided to focus on the web exploitation challenges, as that's where I have the most (albeit limited) experience.

The Challenges

I managed to solve a few of the introductory web challenges. One of them involved a simple SQL injection vulnerability, while another required me to exploit a file inclusion vulnerability. These challenges were a great way to apply the theoretical knowledge I've gained from online courses and labs.

What I Learned

The most important lesson I learned is that CTFs are not just about technical skills. They're also about time management, problem-solving, and perseverance. I also learned the importance of taking good notes and having a systematic approach to tackling challenges.

Conclusion

Overall, my first CTF was a fantastic learning experience. I'm already looking forward to the next one!

Understanding Nmap

Posted on November 15, 2023

Nmap (Network Mapper) is one of the most fundamental tools in the arsenal of any security professional. It's a free and open-source utility for network discovery and security auditing. In this post, we'll take a look at some of the basic and advanced features of Nmap.

Basic Scans

The most basic Nmap scan is a simple TCP connect scan, which can be performed with the `-sT` flag. This scan is reliable, but it's also very noisy and easily detectable. 

nmap -sT 192.168.1.1

Stealth Scans

For more stealthy scanning, you can use a SYN scan (`-sS`), which is often referred to as a "half-open" scan. This type of scan is less likely to be logged by firewalls and intrusion detection systems. 

nmap -sS 192.168.1.1

Version Detection

One of Nmap's most powerful features is its ability to determine the version of the services running on open ports. This can be done with the `-sV` flag. 

nmap -sV 192.168.1.1

Conclusion

Nmap is a versatile and powerful tool with a vast array of options and features. This post has only scratched the surface of what Nmap is capable of. I encourage you to explore the official documentation and experiment with different scan types and options.
